My Design Process

Most of my design process is honestly just building, tweaking, and refining as I go.

I originally got into design through Photoshop and Illustrator —making graphics, edits, and random visual projects. Over time, I started paying more attention to layouts, interfaces, and why certain designs just felt better to use than others.

Now my workflow usually starts with inspiration and experimentation. I build quickly, get feedback, notice friction points, and keep improving from there.

I'm really interested in the balance between visuals and functionality —taking something expressive and making it feel structured, responsive, and intuitive without losing personality.

The Google UX Design Professional Certificate helped me put more structure behind that process, especially around accessibility and usability.
